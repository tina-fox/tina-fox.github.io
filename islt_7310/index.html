Tina Fox

Hi! I’m Tina Fox, and I’m a student in the Learning Technologies and Design master’s program.

I am also an instructional media designer specializing in graphic design and motion graphics for the University of Missouri’s Office of E-Learning. As part of the Course Design and Technology team in Columbia, Missouri, I collaborate with instructional designers, faculty, and other media designers on a wide range of projects, including online and blended courses, videos, handouts, and course quality reviews. It is my goal to help faculty create engaging, meaningful learning experiences for their students and to ensure that students have the best possible experience with their courses. Being an online student in the Learning Technologies and Design program has helped me see things from a student perspective and become a more informed and empathetic media designer.

PROGRAM GOALS

This portfolio demonstrates my ability to meet the Learning Technologies and Design program goals. Each goal contains 1–3 objectives related to mastery of instructional design, technology, collaborative work, research, analytics, and theory, to name a few. For each goal, I have provided examples and evidence of my understanding.

Goal 1 & Objectives

Meeting this goal means that I have successfully designed, developed, managed, and collaborated on theory- and research-based learning systems and experiences to promote meaningful learning.

Goal 2 & Objectives

Meeting this goal means that I have successfully used my analytical skills to collect data, apply theoretical frameworks, and evaluate and reflect on the effectiveness of learning systems that I design.

Goal 3 & Objectives

Meeting this goal means that I have developed the ability to be a socially responsible, reflective, and sensitive technology leader with a strong regard for the intersection of technology and ethics.
